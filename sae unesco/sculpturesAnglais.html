1.Fontana del Porcellino

The Fontana del Porcellino in Florence is a famous little fountain depicting a wild boar. Elle est également connue sous le nom de "Porcellino" en italien.
The fountain is adorned with a bronze statue of a boar, a copy of an ancient work, and is located near the Mercato Nuovo. Visitors are accustomed to rubbing the boar's snout for good luck. It's a popular site in Florence, both for its history and for the tradition associated with the belief in good luck. see more

2. Perseus holding Medusa's head

The sculpture of Perseus holding Medusa's head is an emblematic work of Greek mythology. Created by Italian sculptor Benvenuto Cellini in the 16th century, this bronze statue represents the mythological hero Perseus, holding the severed head of Medusa after defeating her. The work is remarkable for its dynamic realism, capturing the precise moment of Perseus' victory over the mythical monster. It is currently on display at the Bargello National Museum in Florence, Italy. see more

3.Tabernacolo delle Fonticine

The Tabernacolo delle Fonticine is a small artistic structure located in Florence, Italy. Designed by sculptor and architect Giovanni Battista Caccini in the early 17th century, the tabernacle stands at the intersection of Via Cavour and Via dell'Ariento. It is known for its elegant late-Renaissance architectural style and for housing a niche with an artistic representation of the Virgin Mary. Although modest in comparison with some of the other works in the area, the Tabernacolo delle Fonticine is a charming example of Florentine art from this period. see more
