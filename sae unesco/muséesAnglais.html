1. Uffizi Gallery

The Uffizi Museum in Florence is a world-renowned artistic treasure trove. Housed in a historic building known as the "Uffizi", the museum exhibits an exceptional collection of works from the Italian Renaissance. Among the most famous pieces include masterpieces by Botticelli, Titian and Michelangelo. The museum's architecture also offers magnificent views over the city. A must for art lovers visiting Florence. see more

2. Museo Galileo

The Galileo Museum (Museo Galileo) in Florence is dedicated to the history of science and astronomy. It houses a fascinating collection of historic scientific instruments, celestial globes and objects related to the discoveries of Galileo and other renowned scientists. Located in Palazzo Castellani, the museum offers visitors a captivating glimpse into the evolution of scientific knowledge through the centuries. A must-see destination for those interested in the history of scientific thought. see more

3. Museo San Marco or the work of Fra Angelico

The San Marco Museum in Florence is home to the captivating work of Fra Angelico, one of the greatest artists of the Italian Renaissance. Located in a renovated former Dominican monastery, the museum features magnificent frescoes and religious paintings created by Fra Angelico in the 15th century. The spiritually charged works offer an immersive artistic experience in an unspoilt monastic setting, allowing visitors to contemplate Fra Angelico's artistic mastery and profound devotion. A cultural treasure trove for art and history lovers. See more
